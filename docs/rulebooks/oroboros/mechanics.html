The Mechanics

Theme-neutral reference sheet — 5×5 Agon board — 2 players

Components

One 5x5 Agon board (61 hexes). Each player has 5 stones in two colors: 3 of Role A and 2 of Role B, or any 3+2 split agreed before play.

Role 1 (Red)Beats Role 2
Role 2 (Blue)Beats Role 3
Role 3 (Green)Beats Role 4
Role 4 (Gold)Beats Role 1

Player 1 uses Roles 1 and 3. Player 2 uses Roles 2 and 4. Your two roles do not beat each other.

Setup

  • Each player places their 5 stones in any order across their 5-hex starting edge.
  • Player 2 takes one free move before Player 1's first turn.

Turn

Players alternate. Each turn: 2 moves. One move = one stone, one step to an adjacent empty hex. If you hold the center you get 3 moves instead.

If you have no legal move you must pass.

Partnership Rule

A stone may only advance toward center if it starts its move adjacent to at least one friendly stone. Isolated stones may only move sideways or away from center.

Suspended entirely when your opponent holds the center. See Restoration Turn below.

Displacement

Move your stone onto a hex occupied by an enemy stone your role beats. Your stone takes the hex. The displaced stone goes to your opponent's holding area.

Restoration: Spend 1 of your 2 moves to return any held stone to any empty hex on your starting edge. Nothing is permanently lost.

Ko Rule

A stone may not return to a hex it occupied 2 or fewer moves ago.

The Center Hex

State Moves Partnership Displacement
Normal Center empty 2 Required Cycle applies
Holding You hold center 3 (center stone locked) Required for all 3 Any role displaces any role
Restore Opponent holds center 2, partnership suspended Suspended 1 displacement across both moves
Claiming the center: Move any stone onto the center hex. It is now locked — it cannot leave voluntarily. While you hold it, your other stones get 3 moves per turn. The stone holding the center may displace any adjacent enemy regardless of cycle. Any adjacent enemy of any role may also displace it regardless of cycle.

Winning

First to 5 displacement points wins. Every displacement = 1 point regardless of location. If both reach 5 in the same turn: higher total displacement count wins; if equal, later scorer wins; if simultaneous, Player 2 wins.

Playtest Variables

  • Win target: try 4 (shorter) or 6 (longer)
  • Center bonus: try 2 moves instead of 3 if center feels too dominant
  • Restoration turn displacement limit: try 2 if dislodging center is too hard
  • Partnership rule: relax to "within 2 steps of a friendly" if opening is too slow
  • Player 2 free move: remove if Player 2 wins too often
  • Ko rule: extend to 3 moves if stalling persists
